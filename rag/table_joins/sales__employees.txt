# Sales - Employees Join

## Join Relationship
**sales.employee_id = employees.id**

## Join Type  
Many-to-One (Many sales can be made by one employee)

## Join Purpose
Track individual sales performance and commission calculations for sales representatives.

## Common Join Queries
```sql
-- Sales with employee information
SELECT s.sale_date, s.total_amount, s.commission, s.region, s.sale_type,
       e.name as sales_rep, e.position, d.name as department
FROM sales s
JOIN employees e ON s.employee_id = e.id
JOIN departments d ON e.department_id = d.id

-- Employee sales performance and commission tracking
SELECT e.name as sales_rep, COUNT(s.id) as total_sales_count,
       SUM(s.total_amount) as total_sales_revenue,
       SUM(s.commission) as total_commission_earned,
       AVG(s.total_amount) as avg_sale_amount
FROM employees e
LEFT JOIN sales s ON e.id = s.employee_id  
WHERE e.department_id = (SELECT id FROM departments WHERE name = 'Sales')
GROUP BY e.id, e.name

-- Regional sales performance by employee
SELECT e.name as sales_rep, s.region, COUNT(s.id) as sales_count,
       SUM(s.total_amount) as regional_sales, SUM(s.commission) as regional_commission
FROM employees e
JOIN sales s ON e.id = s.employee_id
GROUP BY e.id, e.name, s.region
ORDER BY regional_sales DESC
```

## Key Insights Available
- Individual sales rep performance
- Commission tracking and payouts
- Regional sales territory analysis
- Sales methodology effectiveness (Direct vs Online vs Partner)
- Employee productivity metrics
